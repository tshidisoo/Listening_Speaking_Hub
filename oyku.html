Read the full conversation — play both roles!

Assistant: It's twenty pounds.

You: And how much are these black jeans?

You: Medium, please. Thank you!

🔄 Now vs Every Day

Grammar: present simple (routine) vs present continuous (right now) · I usually… / I am …ing now

🎯 Key Contrast
💡 Present Simple = habits & routines → I usually work in the morning.
💡 Present Continuous = right now → I am sitting at home right now.
📝 Read These 5 Pairs of Sentences

Notice the difference — routine vs. right now!

  • I usually drink tea in the morning. → Right now, I am drinking water.
  • She usually works at an office. → Today, she is working at home.
  • They usually watch TV in the evening. → Now, they are reading books.
  • I usually go to school by bus. → Today, I am walking.
  • He usually cooks dinner. → Right now, he is eating pizza!
🗣 Now YOU speak — two parts! Part 1: What do you USUALLY do in the evening?
Part 2: What are you doing RIGHT NOW at this moment?
Try to use both tenses clearly!
🎙 Record Your Voice

Read the pairs, then speak about YOUR routines vs right now! 🔄
